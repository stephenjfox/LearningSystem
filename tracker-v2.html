The Learning System

From First Principles to High-Yield Synthesis

Stopwatch Pomodoro (25m)
00:00
  • Phase 1: Concept Deconstruction
  • 1 Scope & Definition Understand
    Select a high-yield topic. Define the boundaries: What is this *not*? Identify the "First Principles" mechanism driving the pathology.
  • 2 Mechanism Mapping Analyze
    Sketch the physiological pathway. Identify the exact break point in the chain. If you can't draw the mechanism, you cannot write the question.
  • Phase 2: Vignette Construction
  • 3 The NBME Vignette Synthesize
    Draft the patient story. Use Demographics, Duration, & Dynamics (symptoms). Avoid buzzwords; describe the finding (e.g., "staccato cough" instead of "pertussis").
  • 4 Distractor Logic Evaluate
    Generate 4 wrong answers. Each must be the correct answer for a different disease in the differential.
  • Phase 3: Taxonomy Audit
  • 5 The "Two-Step" Check Analyze
    Does the question require identifying the diagnosis first, then answering a secondary question (e.g., side effect of the treatment)?
  • 6 Skew Difficulty Evaluate
    Verify the cognitive load. Is this purely recall (bad) or does it require evaluating competing data (good)? Aim for Negative Skew (Harder).
